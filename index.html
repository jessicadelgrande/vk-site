VentureKids

A not-for-profit that turns youth from Toronto's underserved neighbourhoods into entrepreneurs

We are building the next generation of entrepreneurs

What we teach

Basic coding fundamentals are no longer optional for high school graduates who want to excel in the modern workforce. Every child won’t grow up to be a developer, but the ability to understand how computers and AI work is important in almost every industry – and becoming more so every year.

Product Building

Building a successful business is about more than just creating a new product. It’s about solving a problem. We help entrepreneurs refine a plan so they can create a business that works.

Financial Literacy

Lack of financial literacy is one of the top reasons small business and startups fail. We teach entrepreneurs the financial fundamentals (revenue, cash flow and investment) so they can thrive.

Web Development 101

Coding is one of today's most in-demand skills. We help students learn introductory HTML, CSS and javascript to help them understand how to build and maintain a website.

Growth Hacking

Growth hacking is more than just a buzzword. The ability to scale and grow can make or break a startup. We help entrepreneurs implement growth strategies, create marketing strategies and foster innovation.

Why our work matters

We work very hard to ensure youth from all backgrounds have a chance to succeed and believe the best technology reflects the community who use it.

The ugly truth about tech's diversity problem

Tech companies control almost every facet of daily life, from how we communicate to the ways in which we travel and, even, how we buy our groceries. Their power is seemingly infinite, which is all the reason more why they must make a concerted effort to champion diverse voices from within.

Diversity isn’t just important for the tech itself—it’s important for the people who make and use technology.

Only 2% of startups are founded by POC

Creating more diversity in the tech space will result in not only better products but more opportunities for growth

Women of colour make up 0.2% of venture deals

Women also start companies at twice the rate of men, yet comprise only 16% of tech founders.

Only 6% of tech firms have a female CEO

Women are underrepresented in senior executive positions at tech-focused companies.
